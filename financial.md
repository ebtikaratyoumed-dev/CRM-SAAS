# Financial Features for Construction CRM

Here is a curated list of the most valuable financial features you can add to your B2B SaaS app for construction companies. These features will give owners clear visibility into their spending, project-level profitability, and overall company financial health.

## 1. Advanced Job Costing (Project-Level Financials)
Construction profitability lives or dies at the project level. Owners need to know if a specific job is making or losing money in real-time.
*   **Budget vs. Actuals Tracking:** Allow users to input their estimated budget for a project (broken down by categories like Labor, Materials, Equipment, and Subcontractors) and compare it against actual spending as the project progresses.
*   **Real-time Project Profitability:** A live gauge showing the current margin on a project.
*   **Change Order Management:** A dedicated flow for handling client change orders, ensuring the extra costs and new revenue are immediately reflected in the project's financial forecast.
*   **Cost to Complete Forecast:** An automatic calculation showing how much money is estimated to be needed to finish the project based on the current burn rate.

## 2. Company-Wide Financial Dashboard (The Owner's View)
An executive dashboard that aggregates all project data into a high-level view of the company's financial health.
*   **Cash Flow Forecasting:** A timeline showing expected cash inflows (upcoming client invoice due dates) versus expected cash outflows (upcoming supplier/subcontractor bills and payroll).
*   **Aggregate Profit Margins:** Overall Gross and Net Profit margins across all active projects.
*   **Accounts Receivable (A/R) & Accounts Payable (A/P):** A summary of who owes the company money (and how late they are) and who the company owes.
*   **Revenue Pipeline:** Integrating the CRM side to project future revenue based on the probability of closing deals currently in the pipeline.

## 3. Expense & Procurement Management
Tracking where the money is going, right from the field.
*   **Supplier Invoice & Receipt Parsing:** Since you already have manual invoice entry, consider adding an OCR feature that automatically reads uploaded supplier invoices or receipts from the field and logs the expenses against the correct project.
*   **Purchase Order (PO) System:** Allow project managers to generate POs. When the supplier invoice arrives, the app can match it against the PO to prevent overbilling.
*   **Subcontractor Payouts (Retainage):** Features to track subcontractor invoices and manage "retainage" (holding back a percentage of payment until the project is completed satisfactorily).

## 4. Labor Cost & Time Tracking Integration
Labor is often the hardest cost to predict and track accurately in construction.
*   **Timesheet-to-Cost Conversion:** Allow field workers to clock in and out of specific projects. The app automatically multiplies those hours by their specific loaded labor rate (including taxes and insurance) to update the project's labor cost live.
*   **Overtime Alerts:** Notifications for project managers when a crew is approaching overtime, which drastically impacts profit margins.

## 5. Automated Reporting & Accounting Integrations
Construction owners need reports for their banks, investors, and tax purposes.
*   **WIP (Work In Progress) Reporting:** A critical construction accounting report that calculates over/under billings based on the percentage of completion. 
*   **Profitability by Dimension:** Analytics that show profitability not just by project, but by *Project Type* (e.g., Commercial vs. Residential), *Client*, or *Project Manager*, helping the owner know where their most profitable work comes from.
*   **Accounting Sync (QuickBooks/Xero):** A feature to sync approved expenses, invoices, and payments directly to their main accounting software so they don't have to double-enter data.

### Recommendation
Start with **Budget vs. Actuals** at the project level, combined with a **Company-Wide Financial Dashboard** that sums up the active projects. This provides the most immediate value for a construction company owner.
